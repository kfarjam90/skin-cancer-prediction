Advanced Skin Lesion Classification

Upload an image of a skin lesion to detect potential malignancies

About the Project

This web application uses a deep learning model trained on dermatoscopic images to identify various skin conditions, with particular focus on malignant lesions:

  • Actinic keratoses and intraepithelial carcinoma / Bowen's disease (akiec)
  • Basal cell carcinoma (bcc)
  • Benign keratosis-like lesions (bkl): solar lentigines, seborrheic keratoses, lichen-planus like keratoses
  • Dermatofibroma (df)
  • Melanoma (mel)
  • Melanocytic nevi (nv)
  • Vascular lesions (vasc): angiomas, angiokeratomas, pyogenic granulomas and hemorrhage

The model was trained on high-quality dermatoscopic images to assist in early detection of skin malignancies, helping users identify potential concerns that warrant professional medical evaluation.

Important Disclaimer

This tool is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of a qualified healthcare provider with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition.

How to get the best results:

  1. Use clear, well-lit photos of the skin lesion
  2. Take the photo at close range but keep the entire lesion in frame
  3. Use a neutral background when possible
  4. Ensure the lesion is in focus with minimal shadows
  5. Include a ruler or scale for size reference if possible

When to see a doctor:

Consult a dermatologist if you notice any lesion that:

  • Is changing in size, shape, or color
  • Is asymmetrical or has irregular borders
  • Has multiple colors
  • Is larger than 6mm (pencil eraser size)
  • Itches, bleeds, or won't heal
